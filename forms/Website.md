ABOUT US

**Empowering Futures, One Mind At a Time - Together, We're Shaping Brighter Tomorrows Through Education.**

Established in 2013 by the late **Mr. Vivek Ponkshe** and his students, Gyan-Setu promotes national integration through educational and cultural outreach. As an initiative of **Jnana Prabodhini's Educational Activity Research Centre (EARC)**, we conduct hands-on science workshops in schools across remote, underserved and aspirational regions of India, including developmentally challenged states and UTs like Arunachal Pradesh, Assam, Nagaland, Manipur, Meghalaya, Chhattisgarh, Odisha, Jharkhand, Madhya Pradesh, Ladakh and Jammu & Kashmir.  
By connecting youth volunteers from different parts of the country with diverse communities, Gyan-Setu fosters mutual respect, emotional bonds, and a shared sense of national identity.  
Join us in spreading the joy of learning and building human bridges across India. Let's connect through knowledge exchange-and grow together!

**Our Story**

At Gyan-Setu, our journey began in 2013 with a visionary idea: to promote national integration through science popularization. We set out to bridge educational gaps in India's remote, tribal, and developmentally challenged regions, using science as a tool to connect, empower, and inspire.

As an initiative of Jnana Prabodhini's Educational Activity Research Centre (EARC), we have conducted hands-on science and mathematics workshops in schools located in some of the country's most underserved, aspirational and culturally diverse regions. From the tranquil hills of Arunachal Pradesh and Meghalaya to the vibrant cultures of Assam, Manipur, and Nagaland, and from the rugged terrains of Chhattisgarh, Odisha, Jharkhand, Madhya Pradesh, and to the remote schools of Ladakh, Jammu & Kashmir, our efforts span across landscapes and communities.

Driven by the spirit of volunteerism, Gyan-Setu brings together passionate individuals - mostly college students and young professionals - from across India. These volunteers not only teach, but also learn, live, and grow with the communities they serve. Through language exchange, homestays, traditional meals, and shared experiences, they form lasting human bonds and develop a deep understanding of India's social and cultural fabric.

With the support of local partner organizations, we continue to build human bridges through knowledge exchange-nurturing curiosity, compassion, and confidence in young learners while transforming volunteers into socially responsible, culturally aware changemakers for the nation.

As we grow, we invite you to join this movement. Together, let's create a future where every child-no matter where they live-has access to joyful learning and a sense of belonging in a united India.

**What We Do**

"Gyan-Setu" is a program conducted by volunteers in remote parts of India. This year-long program,  
organized by Educational Activity Research Center (EARC) - Jnana Prabodhini - Pune, consists of visits  
by teams of volunteers to conduct joyful activity-based learning workshops and camps for middle school students.

- Conduct joyful hands-on science and maths workshop for middle school students
- Build a network of people and grassroot organizations to strengthen this "Knowledge Bridge"
- Organize volunteer visits to remote and aspirational parts of India with an aim of National Integration
